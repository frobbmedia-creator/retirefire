### Content & feature gaps (opportunities)

1. **Coast FIRE + sequence-of-returns** as a single narrative — almost nobody explains *why* a simple coast number can fail if returns are lumpy.
2. **Barista vs Coast side-by-side** with same assumptions and clear definitions (confusion is high in Reddit/YouTube).
3. **Age-by-age Coast tables** with transparent assumptions (linkable assets for backlinks).
4. **“Why simple calculators fail”** educational content that builds trust *without* bashing peers.
5. **Shareable scenario URLs** + CSV export (we have share; CSV shipping Phase 1).
6. **Free forever core** while ProjectionLab/Boldin monetize complexity — freemium must not gut free value.
7. **Mobile-first calculator UX** — many legacy tools are desktop-era.

### Positioning statement (internal)

> RetireFire is the **transparent, risk-aware FIRE calculator destination** for people who already distrust black boxes. Start with clean Coast/Barista/FIRE math; graduate to sequence stress tests without losing the free core.

### Keyword cluster strategy

1. **Tool cluster (now):** Coast, Barista, FIRE number, Years — optimize titles/H1/meta + internal links.
2. **Differentiator cluster (Week 3–6):** sequence risk, Monte Carlo, “simple calculators fail,” Coast SOR.
3. **Linkable data cluster (Week 5–8):** age tables, 2026 realistic numbers, comparison tables.
4. **Trust cluster (ongoing):** methodology, approach, SWR, disclaimers (conversion & brand).

## Competitive takeaways for product

1. **Do not try to out-feature ProjectionLab** in Year 1. Out-clarify and out-educate.
2. **Stress-test MVP** is the wedge that separates us from coastfirecalc-style clones.
3. **Keep free tier excellent** — community backlash if core Coast becomes paywalled.
4. **Publish assumptions aggressively** — few competitors make methodology a product surface.
5. **Reddit/X distribution** beats cold SEO alone for FIRE niche; pair every piece with community-native value (not spam links).
